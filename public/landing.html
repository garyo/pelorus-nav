Open-source marine chartplotter · beta

The friendly, easy-to-use, open-source chartplotter.

Pelorus Nav is a free chartplotter for the browser and Android — and whatever comes next: official NOAA vector charts with standard marine symbology, offline tide and current predictions, no account needed. It's young — in beta, improving steadily, and built in the open by a sailor.

Beta software — not for navigation. Pelorus Nav is young and under active development. Please use it as a planning and situational-awareness aid alongside official charts and proper seamanship — never as your only source of navigation information.

What's aboard today

Official NOAA electronic navigational charts — the same public ENC data behind commercial plotters — rendered with standard marine symbology, on hardware you already own.

Official NOAA charts

Vector ENC coverage of US waters — depths, buoys, lights with their real characteristics — drawn with standard S-52 marine symbology. More regions and bring-your-own charts are on the roadmap.

Works offline

Download a region once and charts, tides, and place search keep working without a signal — handy where cell coverage fades on the water.

Tides & currents

Predictions computed on the device from NOAA harmonic stations — thousands of tide and current stations, shown right on the chart, no internet needed.

The essentials, on deck

Routes, waypoints, track recording, course projection, and a crew-overboard button — laid out to be usable with wet hands on a moving deck.

Any GPS you've got

The device's own GPS works out of the box, and Bluetooth GPS pods pair in a couple of taps. Signal K and NMEA connections are in there too — early, and still lightly tested.

Day, dusk, night — and e-ink

Marine display themes for real conditions, including one tuned for sunlight-readable e-ink tablets.

Made for sailors without a nav station

Day-sailors, club racers, charterers, dinghy cruisers — if your "chartplotter" is the phone in your pocket, this is for you.

  • Free at the core. The app and NOAA charts are free. Optional extras may come later — things like automatic chart updates or syncing between your devices — to help support development; the free core stays free.
  • No account, no tracking. Your position and tracks stay on your device.
  • Offline-first. Designed from the start to keep working when the signal doesn't.
  • Keep your phone dry. Works nicely on an inexpensive waterproof Android or e-ink tablet as a cockpit display.

Built in the open

Pelorus Nav is MIT-licensed and developed in public — the code, the chart pipeline, the roadmap, the mistakes. You can read it, file issues, build your own, or self-host the whole thing.

Being open source is also a promise of continuity: the code and the free charts will be there for good, whatever comes next for the project.
