Open-source marine chartplotter

Real charts. Your own device. No subscription.

Pelorus Nav turns the phone or tablet you already own into a serious chartplotter: official NOAA vector charts, real marine symbology, tides and currents — fully offline, free forever, built in the open by a sailor.

Runs in any modern browser — nothing to install, no account. iOS is in the works.

Boston Inner Harbor, live in the app — NOAA ENC data with IHO S-52 symbology, street basemap blended in near shore.

A real chartplotter, not a map with boats

The same NOAA electronic navigational charts that commercial plotters license — public data, rendered properly, on hardware you already own.

Official NOAA charts

Full vector ENC coverage of US waters — depths, buoys, lights with their real characteristics, restricted areas, cables — drawn with authentic S-52 marine symbology. More regions and bring-your-own charts are on the roadmap.

Works offline, properly

Download a region once and everything works with the radio off: charts, tides, place search. Cell coverage ends at the harbor mouth; your charts shouldn't.

Tides & currents, no internet

Predictions computed on the device from NOAA harmonic stations — thousands of tide and current stations, right on the chart, offshore or not.

Navigation that earns its keep

Routes, waypoints, track recording, course projection, anchor and crew-overboard one-press response — designed for use with wet hands on a bouncing deck.

Any GPS you've got

The device's own GPS works out of the box. Bluetooth GPS pods, NMEA and Signal K boat networks plug in when you want boat-grade data.

Day, dusk, night — and e-ink

Proper marine display themes, including one tuned for sunlight-readable e-ink tablets — the budget cockpit display that never washes out.

Made for sailors without a nav station

Day-sailors, club racers, charterers, dinghy cruisers — if your "chartplotter" is the phone in your pocket, this is for you.

  • Nothing to buy, nothing to subscribe to. The app and every NOAA chart are free, forever. That's a promise the license keeps: it's open source.
  • No account, no tracking. Your position and tracks live on your device and nowhere else.
  • Take it offshore. Offline-first isn't a feature tier — it's the whole design.
  • Keep your phone dry. Pairs beautifully with a cheap waterproof Android or e-ink tablet as a dedicated cockpit display.

Built in the open

Pelorus Nav is MIT-licensed and developed in public — the code, the chart pipeline, the roadmap, the mistakes. You can read it, file issues, build your own, or self-host the whole thing.

Openness isn't a marketing line here; it's the guarantee that this app can't be enshittified out from under you.
