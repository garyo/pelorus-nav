# Pelorus Nav User Guide

Pelorus Nav is a free, open-source chartplotter that runs in your browser and
as an app on Android and iOS. It shows official NOAA vector charts with
IHO S-52 symbology, works fully offline once you've downloaded chart regions,
and handles the essentials of small-boat navigation: routes, waypoints,
tracks, tides and currents, and man-overboard.

![Pelorus Nav showing Boston Harbor](/images/overview.png)

## Where to get it

- **Web / PWA**: [pelorus-nav.com/app](https://pelorus-nav.com/app) — works in
  any modern browser, and can be installed to your home screen for full-screen,
  offline use.
- **Android**: available from the Play Store, or as an APK attached to each
  [GitHub release](https://github.com/garyo/pelorus-nav/releases).
- **iOS**: via TestFlight (beta).

## This guide

- [Getting Started](/getting-started) — first launch, settings worth changing,
  and downloading charts for offline use.
- [The Chart Display](/chart-display) — chart modes, detail levels, layers,
  and the instrument display.
- [Routes](/routes) — creating, editing, and following routes.
- [Tracks](/tracks) — recording where you went, and reliving it in the
  Track Viewer.
- [Tides, Currents, Wind & Sun](/environment) — predicted conditions on
  the chart, up to 48 hours ahead.

The guide covers the interesting parts, not every menu item — most of the app
is meant to be discoverable by tapping around, and nothing you do in a panel
or menu is destructive without confirmation.

::: warning Not for primary navigation
Pelorus Nav is a planning and situational-awareness aid. Always maintain a
proper lookout and consult official publications. Charts can be out of date
and GPS can fail — never rely on a single source of navigation information.
:::
